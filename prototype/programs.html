Our Programs

Highly-individualized wraparound services that address the root causes of homelessness and empower single mothers to build stable, independent lives for their families.

Our Approach

A holistic path to lasting independence

Saint Louise House serves single mothers with dependent children who are experiencing homelessness and need more than what emergency shelters can provide. Many of our families face complex, overlapping challenges -- from housing instability and financial hardship to gaps in education and health care.

Our highly-individualized approach goes beyond temporary relief. We provide wraparound services across five core areas -- Stable Housing, Education, Financial Literacy, Family Relationships, and Healthy Living -- so that every mother has the foundation and tools she needs to break the cycle of homelessness for herself and her children.

Each family works with a dedicated Empowerment Partner to create a personalized plan that addresses their unique circumstances, strengths, and goals. This integrated model ensures that no family falls through the cracks.

What We Offer

Programs designed for lasting change

Every program is built around the understanding that stable families need more than a roof over their heads -- they need comprehensive support to thrive.

Stable Housing

Safe, affordable housing is the foundation for all lasting change. Saint Louise House provides families with a secure home so mothers can focus on building the skills and stability they need. Our housing removes the constant stress of homelessness and gives families the breathing room to plan for the future, pursue employment, and engage with our full range of support services.

Education & Financial Literacy

Financial independence starts with knowledge. Our education programs help mothers earn their GED, develop job-readiness skills, and build confidence in the workplace. Alongside these efforts, our financial literacy curriculum covers budgeting, saving, credit repair, and long-term financial planning -- equipping families with the tools to achieve and sustain economic self-sufficiency.

Family & Wellness

Healthy families are the heart of everything we do. We offer individual and group counseling, parenting education, and dedicated children's programs that support emotional and developmental growth. Our wellness initiatives address physical health, mental well-being, and healthy relationship skills -- giving mothers and children the resilience they need to face the future with confidence.

How It Works

The pathway to independence

From the moment you reach out, our team walks alongside you through every stage of the journey.

Apply

Reach out by phone or through our online application. Our intake team will schedule an interview to learn about your family's situation, assess eligibility, and answer any questions you have about the program. There is no cost to apply.

Get Matched

Once accepted, you are paired with a dedicated Empowerment Partner who works with you to create a personalized plan. Together, you set goals across housing, education, finances, and wellness -- and we match you with the specific services and resources your family needs.

Build Your Future

With stable housing secured and your support plan in place, you begin working toward your goals with ongoing guidance from our team. The length of stay is tailored to each family's needs, giving you the time to gain the skills, stability, and confidence to transition to fully independent living.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Who is eligible for Saint Louise House programs?

Our programs serve single mothers with dependent children who are currently experiencing homelessness in the Austin, Texas area. Applicants must demonstrate a willingness to actively participate in Empowerment Partner meetings and work toward personal goals. We prioritize families who need more than what emergency shelters can provide and are ready to commit to building long-term stability.

How long does the program last?

Program length is tailored to each family's needs. The duration depends on individual progress toward goals in areas such as employment, education, financial stability, and independent housing readiness. Our team works with each mother to set a realistic timeline and adjust as needed.

Is there a cost to participate?

There is no cost to apply. Families in the program pay a small, graduated portion of their income toward rent as part of building financial responsibility and preparing for independent living. This amount is set collaboratively with your Empowerment Partner and is designed to be manageable while encouraging savings and budgeting habits.

What support is available for my children?

Children are at the center of our mission. We offer age-appropriate programming that includes after-school tutoring, mentorship, developmental assessments, and enrichment activities. Our goal is to ensure that every child is thriving academically, socially, and emotionally -- setting them up for long-term success alongside their mothers.

Can I continue my education while in the program?

Absolutely. We actively encourage and support continuing education. Whether you are working toward your GED, attending community college, or pursuing vocational training, our Empowerment Partners help you access educational resources, scholarships, and flexible scheduling so you can balance school, parenting, and program participation.

How do I apply?

You can begin the application process by calling us at 512.302.0027 or by visiting our online application page. Our intake team will walk you through the process, answer your questions, and schedule an initial interview. We encourage you to reach out even if you are unsure about eligibility -- we are happy to help.
