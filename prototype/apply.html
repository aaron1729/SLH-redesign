You have already taken a brave step by seeking help. Saint Louise House is here to walk alongside you and your children on the path to stability and independence.

Who We Serve

Eligibility requirements

Our program is designed for families who need more than emergency shelter -- mothers and children who are ready for a supportive, long-term path toward self-sufficiency.

  • Single mothers with dependent children
  • Currently experiencing homelessness or at imminent risk of homelessness
  • Willing to actively participate in program services and case management
  • Committed to setting and working toward personal growth goals
  • Located in or able to relocate to the Austin / Travis County area
What to Expect

The application process

We want the process to feel manageable and welcoming. Here is what happens after you reach out.

Initial Contact

Call our Program Office or fill out the form below. A staff member will follow up within two business days to learn more about your situation.

Assessment

Meet with our intake team for a confidential conversation about your needs, goals, and family circumstances so we can determine the best fit.

Enrollment

Once accepted, you will be matched with housing and a personalized service plan that includes case management, education, and family wellness support.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions about applying

Is there a cost to participate in the program?

Saint Louise House does not charge rent during the initial program phase. As families progress, they may begin contributing a small portion of their income to build financial habits, but no one is ever turned away because of an inability to pay.

How long does the program last?

The length of stay varies based on each family's needs and goals. Most families participate in the program for 12 to 24 months, giving them enough time to build the skills, savings, and support network needed for lasting independence.

Can I apply if I have a son over a certain age?

We welcome mothers with dependent children of all ages. If you have specific questions about your family's situation, please reach out to our Program Office and we will be happy to discuss your circumstances.

What services are included in the program?

Families receive stable housing along with case management, educational support, financial literacy coaching, family wellness programs, children's enrichment activities, and connections to community resources. Every service plan is tailored to the individual family.

Do I need to have a referral to apply?

No referral is required. You can contact us directly by phone or by filling out the form on this page. We also accept referrals from partner organizations, shelters, and social workers.

What happens after I submit my inquiry?

A member of our intake team will reach out within two business days to schedule an initial conversation. From there, we will guide you through the assessment process and let you know about next steps.
