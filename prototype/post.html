Celebrating 500 Families Served

January 15, 2026

January 15, 2026 · 4 min read

Featured Image

This month, Saint Louise House reached a milestone that once felt like a distant dream: we have now served 500 families through our transitional housing and comprehensive support programs. Since our founding, every family who has walked through our doors has carried a unique story of hardship, resilience, and hope. Today, we pause to honor each of those 500 families and the strength they have shown on their journey toward stability.

The road to this moment has been shaped by the collective effort of our staff, volunteers, donors, and community partners. From providing safe, affordable housing to offering case management, counseling, education programs, and childcare support, our holistic approach addresses the root causes of homelessness. Over the years, we have witnessed mothers earn GEDs, complete workforce training, secure employment, and ultimately move into permanent housing with renewed confidence in their ability to provide for their children.

"Every family who comes to us is already resilient. Our job is to remove the barriers standing between them and the independent, stable lives they deserve. Over 90% of our families maintain stability and increase self-sufficiency -- that's the power of stable housing combined with wraparound support."

-- Laura Ward, Executive Director, Saint Louise House

Looking Ahead: Our Next Chapter

While we celebrate this achievement, we also recognize that the need in Austin continues to grow. The number of families experiencing homelessness in Central Texas has risen steadily, and the waiting list for our program remains long. In the coming year, we plan to expand our capacity by adding ten new housing units, launching an enhanced financial literacy curriculum, and deepening partnerships with local employers to create more pathways to living-wage jobs for the mothers in our program.

None of this work is possible without the generosity of our supporters. Whether you have donated, volunteered your time, hosted a supply drive, or simply shared our story with someone who needed to hear it, you are part of this milestone. We invite you to continue standing with us as we work toward a future where every mother and child in Austin has a safe place to call home. If you would like to help us serve the next 500 families, please consider making a donation or getting involved today.
