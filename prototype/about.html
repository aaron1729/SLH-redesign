About Saint Louise House

An Austin, Texas nonprofit empowering women and their children to break the cycle of homelessness for generations.

Our Mission

Why we exist

Saint Louise House serves single mothers and their dependent children experiencing homelessness in Austin, Texas. Many of the families we work with need more comprehensive, long-term support than emergency shelters can provide. We meet them where they are and walk alongside them toward lasting stability.

Empowering women and their children to overcome homelessness for generations.

Our ultimate goal is to break the cycle of poverty and homelessness -- not just for the mothers in our program, but for their children and the generations that follow. By addressing the root causes of housing instability, we create a ripple effect that extends far beyond a single family.

Our Approach

Intense partnership for lasting change

At Saint Louise House, we believe that a safe, stable home is the foundation upon which everything else is built. But housing alone is not enough. That is why our model centers on what we call an intense partnership with each family we serve.

Every mother in our program receives stable, affordable housing paired with a customized set of wraparound services designed to address her unique circumstances and goals. These services include:

  • Education -- GED completion, college enrollment support, vocational training, and academic mentoring for children.
  • Financial literacy -- Budgeting, credit repair, savings plans, and long-term financial goal-setting.
  • Family relationships -- Parenting classes, family counseling, and healthy relationship building.
  • Wellness -- Mental health counseling, substance abuse recovery support, physical health resources, and self-care strategies.

By combining stable housing with this comprehensive, individualized support, we help each family develop the skills, confidence, and resources they need to achieve true independence.

Our Journey

Our history

2000 -- Founded in Austin

Saint Louise House was established to fill a critical gap in services for single mothers and their children experiencing homelessness in Austin, Texas. The organization opened its first transitional housing units with a small but dedicated team of staff and volunteers.

2009 -- Expanded Housing Capacity

Responding to growing demand, Saint Louise House expanded its housing portfolio, enabling more families to receive the stable, long-term support they needed to rebuild their lives.

2014 -- Launched Wraparound Services Model

Building on a decade of experience, the organization formalized its comprehensive wraparound services model, integrating education, financial literacy, wellness, and family support into every participant's individualized plan.

2018 -- Deepened Community Partnerships

Saint Louise House strengthened partnerships with Austin-area employers, educational institutions, and healthcare providers, creating a robust network of resources available to program participants.

Today -- Serving Hundreds of Families

With more than 500 families served since its founding, Saint Louise House continues to grow its impact, refine its programs, and advocate for systemic solutions to family homelessness in Central Texas.
